class="title_edit"></span>                              <h1>Deviled Eggs with Tarragon</h1>                                        <div class="clear"></div>        </div>                                <div id="recipe_flag" style="display:none">                      </div>                <div id="rating_module">          <img alt="CHOW" src="http://www.chow.com/images/recipes/icon_chow.20080408224935.gif" />                            </div><!-- .ratings_module -->                          <div id="photos">                          <img alt="" src="http://www.chow.com/assets/2007/05/deviledeggs_290.20080408224935.jpg" />                      </div>                        <div id="time" class="with_image">                      <img alt="Difficulty: Easy" src="http://www.chow.com/images/recipes/difficulty_easy.20080408224935.jpg" title="Difficulty: Easy" />                                          <div id="servings">              <p class="header">TIME/SERVINGS</p>        <p><strong>Total:</strong> 1 hr</p>        <p><strong>Active:</strong> 15 mins</p>        <p><strong>Makes:</strong> 12 deviled eggs</p>            </div>                              <div class="author">                          <br /><a href="http://www.chow.com/profile/48651"><img alt="" class="avatar tiny" src="/uploads/4/4/6/147644_t32_tiny.jpg" /></a>&nbsp;By Kate Ramos                      </div>                            </div>                <div class="clear"></div>                                                                  <p><strong>                  </strong></p>                <div id="introduction">                      <div id='intro_full'><p>Who doesn&#8217;t love chilled, creamy deviled eggs? The addition of tarragon and capers to our recipe creates the perfect foil for the silky, rich yolks.</p></div>                  </div>                          <div class="header_section"><span>INGREDIENTS</span></div>          <div id="ingredients">            <ul><li>6 large eggs</li><li>1/4 cup mayonnaise</li><li>1 teaspoon Dijon <a href="http://www.chow.com/ingredients/310">mustard</a></li><li>1 teaspoon minced <a href="http://www.chow.com/ingredients/273">capers</a></li><li>1 1/2 teaspoons minced fresh <a href="http://www.chow.com/ingredients/225">tarragon</a> leaves</li><li>2 tablespoons minced <a href="http://www.chow.com/ingredients/160">chives</a></li></ul>          </div>                        <div class="header_section"><span>INSTRUCTIONS</span></div>        <div id="instructions">                    <ol>    <li class="d1">Place eggs in a medium saucepan in a single layer and add enough cold water to cover eggs by 1 inch. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then lower heat to maintain a gentle simmer. Simmer for 9 minutes, then carefully drain eggs, running under cold water until cool enough to handle. Let cool in the refrigerator for 20 minutes.</li>        <li class="d2">Crack and peel each hard-cooked egg. With a sharp knife, slice eggs in half lengthwise. Scoop out yolks and place in a medium mixing bowl; reserve empty egg halves on a serving tray. Once all eggs have been yolked, cover the whites and transfer the tray to the refrigerator.</li>        <li class="d3">Mash yolks with a fork and add mayonnaise, mustard, capers, and tarragon. Mix thoroughly with a fork until smooth. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.</li>        <li class="d4">Remove egg white halves from the refrigerator and fill each with 1 heaping teaspoon of yolk mixture, rounding the tops with a spoon. Garnish each with a sprinkling of minced chives and serve.</li>    </ol>        </div>                <p class="comments_header">          COMMENTS                      |&nbsp;<a href="#" onclick="new Effect.ScrollTo('comments_reply', { offset: -200 }); return false;">ADD YOUR OWN</a>                  </p>      </div>            <div class="clear"></div>            <div id="comments">  <a name="comments"></a>  <div id="all-comments">    <div id="comment-list">              <div id="comment-6144" class="post">  <a name="comment-6144"></a>        <p class="post_content">    lovely  </p>  <p class="post_info">    <a href="http://www.chow.com/profile/55914"><img alt="" class="avatar tiny" src="/images/avatar-tiny.gif" /></a>    <strong><a href="http://www.chow.com/profile/55914">marlie202</a></strong>    &nbsp;|&nbsp;    May 18, 2007 12:32PM      </p></div><div id="comment-7636" class="post">  <a name="comment-7636"></a>        <p class="post_content">    Huge hit with everyone who has tried them.
